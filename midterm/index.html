Explore Nanjing

Nanjing is located on the eastern side of China, which reaches the Yangtze River, where the city is cheery and celebrated, for it is known to be one of China’s most important cities in history and culture. The city served as a capital during multiple dynasties and governments. Nanjing remains rich in its cultural heritage, grand city walls, and Ming-era architecture that reveals old traditions and histories within the city. Nanjing has always been a representation of China’s politics and history for over a millennium. Today, the city is known for its modern culture, education, and invention while holding and blending its historical tradition and past to create a vibrant city.

Quick Highlights

  • Nanjing's name means "Southern Capital" in Chinese.
  • The city is the home location of the Ming Xiaoling Mausoleum, a UNESCO World Heritage site.
  • The city is known for its food in Jinling cuisine, such as Nanjing’s salted duck.
  • Nanjing has been the capital of China multiple times, such as during the Ming Dynasty and the Republic of China era.
