To begin, the difference between structured content and semantic HTML is that structured content is formed by modular chunks, while semantic HTML explains the hierarchy and relationships of the chunks. As explained by the article, structured content is not a finished page, but instead a collection of important and meaningful data - such as title, recipe instructions, product descriptions, etc. - that breaks everything down for each chunk to have a purpose and relationship with other pieces, which creates the overall content clear and crucial. Next, Semantic HTML is the markup that conveys these relationships, for it doesn't just control and maintain how the connection should look on screen but also expresses its purpose and structure. For example, using < h1 > and < h2 > tags shows hierarchy and conveys the significance of the headings. Or by using < li > within < ol > tags, arranges the list of items in a format that can be realized both by machines and humans to be overstepped and interpreted. Therefore, structured content and semantic HTML work together to create unity. Structured breaks down the data in modular, purposeful units that can be used again across contexts. Then, semantic HTML will communicate the reliability of the chunks to enforce and ensure that they can be usable and understandable to both the machines and viewers. Therefore, when structured content creates reusable and meaningful information, and semantic HTML rearranges their meanings or purpose by making it available to search engines and technological assistance, they create units for both of them to play a major role in form enhancement. Furthermore, it’s important to have structured content and semantic HTML in my web pages to make my website more accessible and advanced. To explain, having structured content with semantic markup would enable search engines to generate rich snippets, such as recipes that would display ingredients or cooking times on the screen while making my search results clear for users. Another example would be having a structured and semantically marked content to enforce my website work across platforms such as apps, desktops, and voice assistance. These things would not only improve the usability for my users but also make my website faster and clearer, causing them to be more adaptable.
