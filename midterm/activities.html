Top Activities in Nanjing

Qinhuai River

Taking a cruise on the Qinhuai River is an enchanting experience to enjoy Nanjing's history and its nightlife. The river is known to be the “mother river” of Nanjing that travels through many ancient bridges, architecture, temples, and the busy and lively Confucius Temple district. The area then transforms during the night as red lanterns glow and reflect against the water, making it sparkle and allowing visitors to enjoy the festival spirit. Visitors can enjoy going to the market, snacks, and local performances that are being held, representing their culture and the warmth in the night.

Ming Xiaoling Mausoleum

The Ming Xiaoling Mausoleum is at the southern slope location of the Purple Mountain, which is the resting place of Emperor Zhu Yuanzhang, who is known to be the founder of the Ming Dynasty. The scenery is known for its long Sacred Way that is lined with stone mythical ancient creatures and animals that protect and guard the emperor's tomb. Nature is heavily blended with the solemn architecture, where visitors can walk along the green path and not the serenity and the view of China’s past. Revealing to the visitors the UNESCO World Heritage Site creating art and strength of the early Ming Dynasty designs.

Nanjing Museum

The Nanjing Museum is known to be one of China’s biggest and oldest museums that has housed over 400,000 cultural relics spanning its thousands of years of history. The museum holds many valuable pieces like jade, porcelain, paintings, art, modern art, and calligraphy that reveal the enhancement and evolution of Chinese civilization throughout history. The museum’s design is a blend of traditional arts and modern architecture that represents the harmony between the old and the new era of China.
